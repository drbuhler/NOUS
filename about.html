Our Name & Philosophy

"Wisdom through repentance."

The nous (Gr. νοῦς) is the heart, mind, or center of man. In the Orthodox Christian tradition, the νοῦς was united to God in the Garden, darkened in the Fall, and is cleansed again through prayer, fasting, almsgiving, liturgical worship, and participation in the sacraments.

Saint Paul urges us to be transformed by the renewing of the νοῦς, so as to reject conformity to a world which is passing away.

This pursuit of noesis or the highest understanding echoes throughout the classical tradition. Plato urged his followers to practice noesis—the contemplation of the divine forms—rather than merely seeking education through arithmetic, music, or the practical arts alone.

At the NOUS network, we believe true education must address the center of the human person. We must not only enrich the mind but restore the νοῦς to its proper orientation toward God and the created logoi of creation.

Our Story

A Brief History

NOUS grew out of lived experience. After five years as founding headmaster of Saint Andrew Academy, a parish school in Riverside, CA, Reader Dionysius (Keith) Buhler, PhD saw both the beauty and the difficulty of building an Orthodox school from scratch — and how much easier the road becomes when founders can draw on the experience of those who have walked it before.

Together with Subdeacon Callum (Benjamin) Lyda of Living Classical, he began connecting the growing but scattered world of Orthodox classical education: parish schools, community schools, and homeschool co-ops across the country, each solving the same problems alone.

National Orthodox United Schools was founded to be the hub those educators lacked — a network for connection, a library of open-source resources, and a channel for grants and expertise. Our aim is the 2035 Mandate: 35 thriving, sustainable Orthodox schools across the US by 2035.

Our motto is "wisdom through repentance" — only by humility, prayer, and spiritual labor can we clean the heart and thus see God and the logoi in creation.
