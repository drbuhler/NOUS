Our Name & Philosophy

"Wisdom through repentance."

The nous (Gr. νοῦς) is the heart, mind, or center of man. In the Orthodox Christian tradition, the νοῦς was united to God in the Garden, darkened in the Fall, and is cleansed again through prayer, fasting, almsgiving, liturgical worship, and participation in the sacraments.

Saint Paul urges us to be transformed by the renewing of the νοῦς, so as to reject conformity to a world which is passing away.

This pursuit of higher understanding echoes throughout the classical tradition. Plato urged his followers to practice noesis—the contemplation of the divine forms—rather than merely seeking education through arithmetic, music, or the practical arts alone.

At the NOUS network, we believe true education must address the center of the human person, restoring the νοῦς to its proper orientation toward God and His truth.
