The Context

Meeting the Moment

As converts pour into the Orthodox Church, the need for stable foundations has never been greater. We need more than just full pews; we need stable homes, a robust clergy, and a new generation of Orthodox teachers.

Currently, many Orthodox parish schools, private schools, and homeschool co-ops exist. But they are challenging to launch, and even harder to maintain. NOUS aims to bridge this gap, providing churches, communities, and families with the tools they need to thrive.

The 2035 Mandate:

35 thriving, sustainable Orthodox classical schools across the US by 2035.

Scalable Models

Build for Success at Every Size

Institutions require different foundations based on their mission and scope.

Small

Homeschool Coop

The Model: Parent-led communities meeting 1–3 days a week | 2-15 families | Focus on supplement and fellowship.

Our Recommendation

Medium

Parish School

The Model: 5-day schools and 3-day schools, often hosted by or connected to a parish) with a majority Orthodox student body and full administrative team | 10-100 families | Focus on professionalization, training, and annual fundraising.

Our Recommendation

Large

Community School

The Model: 5-day, full-service private schools in major metropolitan statistical areas. Our Name & Philosophy

"Wisdom through repentance."

The nous (Gr. νοῦς) is the heart, mind, or center of man. In the Orthodox Christian tradition, the νοῦς was united to God in the Garden, darkened in the Fall, and is cleansed again through prayer, fasting, almsgiving, liturgical worship, and participation in the sacraments.

Saint Paul urges us to be transformed by the renewing of the νοῦς, so as to reject conformity to a world which is passing away.

This pursuit of noesis or the highest understanding echoes throughout the classical tradition. Plato urged his followers to practice noesis—the contemplation of the divine forms—rather than merely seeking education through arithmetic, music, or the practical arts alone.

At the NOUS network, we believe true education must address the center of the human person. We must not only enrich the mind but restore the νοῦς to its proper orientation toward God and the created logoi of creation.
